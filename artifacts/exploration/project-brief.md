# Project Brief: Algorithmic Fairness & Transparency Portal

## Spring 2026 Case Introduction
**Course:** MIS 430

---

## Background

A large, consumer-facing digital platform that provides online services to millions of users across the United States is seeking your help. The organization is facing increasing pressure (from users, regulators, and the public) to be more transparent about how their automated and AI-supported decisions affect people. The platform uses automated and AI-supported decision-making to determine things such as:

1. Which users receive access to certain services or opportunities
2. How requests are prioritized or queued
3. What content, options, or recommendations users see

These decisions do not determine life-or-death outcomes, but they can significantly affect users' experiences, opportunities, and perceptions of fairness. The organization operates in a highly competitive market, where trust and user satisfaction are critical, and it is subject to emerging regulations and public scrutiny around the use of AI and automated systems.

While leadership believes the organization's use of automation is appropriate and valuable, there is growing concern about fairness, explainability, and accountability. The organization has decided to explore a public-facing web-based solution to address these concerns.

---

## The Problem

Leadership agrees that "something needs to be done," but there is no consensus on:

- What level of transparency is appropriate
- Which users should see what information
- How much explanation is possible or desirable
- How fairness should be defined or evaluated
- What role humans should play in reviewing or overriding automated decisions

Different stakeholders hold conflicting priorities, and many details are unclear or undefined.

Your team has been brought in to analyze the situation, gather requirements, and propose a system design that balances organizational goals, ethical considerations, and user needs.

---

## Your Role

You are acting as a consultant engaged to:

- Understand the problem space
- Identify and interview stakeholders
- Elicit and refine requirements over time
- Design and iteratively refine a solution
- Deliver a working website prototype that demonstrates your proposed system

You should assume:

- You do not have direct access to production data
- You may use synthetic or AI-generated sample data where needed
- Requirements may evolve as new information emerges

---

## Initial Constraints (Subject to Change)

At the start of the project:

- The solution must be web-based
- The solution must address fairness and transparency in some meaningful way
- The solution must consider both organizational risk and user trust

Beyond this, very little has been decided.

---

## What to Expect Over the Semester

Throughout the semester:

- You will conduct stakeholder interviews (with role-played stakeholders)
- You will receive new information, constraints, or changes in direction
- You will be asked to justify tradeoffs and design decisions
- You may need to revise assumptions and requirements

This project is intentionally designed to be iterative and ambiguous, mirroring real-world systems development.
